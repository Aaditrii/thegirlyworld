Problems Faced By Women

There are a lot of problems that are faced by women in the society.In earlier times,women were not even allowed in the kitchen during their menstruation period.Women were not allowed to even touch items like pickles or flowers.Although in urban areas,these beliefs have been reduced to a great extent but not finished.Today, even in this 21st century, these beliefs are followed in villages and traditional areas of India.Here are some of the listed problems which affect women most in the society.


1.Female Molestation



Female molestation is one of the biggest problems of not only India but all over the world.According to a 23 year old study of NCBI,participants included 6 to 16-year-old females with substantiated sexual abuse and a demographically similar comparison group. Every day in newspapers or by the means of any other media we listen about the sexual abuse chapters taking place in our surroundings.There are no regular and strong rules for such cases by our constitution even the culprits are given bail and they further harass the victims and their family to take back their case from the court.This keeps growing day by day.There have been many female sexual and molestation cases that took out breath of every girl in the past and present years.The problem is not with anyone but the problem is that the women are thought to be weak and dependent which is absolutely false.If we want a safer country then probably first a woman should know how to protect herself,her surroundings and her confidence.Many solutions can be suggested such as self defense,keeping safety items such as pepper spray etc with them.Also one should speak out and reach out such problems.


2.Female Infanticide



Female infanticide is also one of the biggest problems of India.Boys are considered as the king of the family and the extenders of family and girls are seen as a burden for parents,how cheap this sounds!!.Today well,these beliefs are not followed by most of the families but in many rural areas,this is followed till today.In India,checking the gender of the child before birth is illegal which is a strong and major step towards vanishing female infanticide.Truely said,a daughter is not a tension but she is equal to ten sons.A women can also be a successor of family and a girl can also alone take care of her family financially and economically.This concern should be spread as much as possible.There are many government schemes which support female birth such as Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana : (SSY),Balika Samridhi Yojana: (BSY) ,Mukhyamantri Rajshri Yojana (MRY) and many more .These schemes offers high interest rates as compared to other national schemes,tax savings etc.We all should also take care of our surroundings and try to stop these illegal practises such as female infanticide,child abuse as much as possible.


3.Dowry System



Dowry refers to the bride's riches that are given to the groom and his family during marriage.This is such an irony that a women comes to other family and also brings money and other luxuries for that family.In many cases,if the bride's family is not able to meet the demands of the groom's family then either the marriage gets cancelled or the girl is torchured for whole life for not bringing the riches.Although this is illegal in India but today also the groom's family indirectly ask for dowry and the bride's family is ready to pay because of their daughter's respect.Dowry includes money,furniture,land and many more.I sometimes think that how cheap would be the mind of that person or family who started all this means,a women is coming to a new house leaving her all relations behind and the family is asking money for her coming .Woman is not an item or a doll to be purchased or bought.Every woman has her independent right,her joy of living according to her,marrying on her own wishes.For reducing dowry system,I think women should be much educated to understand and stand against dowry,she should be financially self sufficient and confident so that if she faces such situations then she should take positive and right decision for her.


4.Domestic Violence



Domestic violence refers to torchure done by males mostly on their wives or it can be vice versa too.As we learnt about dowry practice in above paragraph thay if the bride's family is not able to give riches to the groom's family,that women is torchured by her husband and his family which is the main cause of domestic violence.Drinking habits,frustration and negative mental health make the males to torchure females to such an extent that either those females die or commit suicide.A woman in a family also have equal rights such as man have.The other family members such as children and the parents are also affected by domestic violence.According to a National Family and Health Survey in 2005, total lifetime prevalence of domestic violence was 33.5% and 8.5% for sexual violence among women aged 15–49.There are many laws for prevention of domestic violence in india such as DOWRY PROHIBITION ACT in 1961.The most recent legislation is the Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act (PWDVA) 2005. The PWDVA, a civil law, includes physical, emotional, sexual, verbal, and economic abuse as domestic violence.Many women hide domestic violence over them to save the names of their family and themselves and this is wrong.One should raise voice against the culprits such that no another woman faces such.

1.Education


Education and knowledge plays a powerful role in the upbringing of the society.The key to the freedom of women is education.The more educated a women is,the more she is aware about her rights and the more she is aware about the laws,no one can never bow her down.Women education will lead to the reduction of all the evils in this society.Kothari commission of 1968 recommended education as tool for social development.Also when a girl will gain equal education as compared to male then the gender inequality will be vanished.Also one of the major initiatives taken by government of india for education is BETI BACHAO,BETI PADHAO yojana(save daughter,educate daughter).This scheme was launched with an initial funding of ₹100 crore and mainly targets states like UttarPradesh,Bihar,Haryana etc.



NGOs And Organizations

International Women's Day is celebrated on March 8 every year.There are many events and conferences that are held for women empowerment every year.Many conferences held every year internationally are Cataylist Conference,Financial Women's Association International Business Conference and many more.There are a lot of NGOs based organizations that are working for women empowerment.Some of them are listed below:


1. Shanti Sahyog center for women is a NGO based organization which aims for women empowerment and education. Delhi Govt. selected and funded Shanti Sahyog to run a Gender Resource Centre (GRC) Stree Shakti Suvidha Kendra from 1st September 2010 to 31st July 2016.This organization aims to uplift the women belonging to underprivileged sections by teaching computer application and other works.This organization follows the rules of Gandhi ji.Since 2004,Shanti Sahyog has been following their mission to enable people living in underprivileged areas to a better quality of life.A team of 43 social workers are dedicated to serving families in 17 slums in the Kalkaji / Govindpuri and Tughlakabad Village areas of Delhi.
